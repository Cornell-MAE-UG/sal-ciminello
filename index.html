Background
Iron Man made me want to be an engineer.
I wasn't joking.

Elementary school, first viewing of Iron Man — I walked out wanting to build things that help people. I've been chasing that ever since, just with more math and considerably fewer fictional arc reactors.

At Cornell I went from freshman on the magnetic braking subteam to Mechanical Lead of Cornell Hyperloop, running six subteams and ~40 engineers across electromagnetic levitation, composites, braking, and aero. Simultaneously I've been doing M.Eng research at the Cornell Aerospace Adversary Lab on an AFRL-funded CubeSat constellation testbed — structural design, inter-satellite comms, and formation control.

Outside of school I co-founded Go2, a portable carbonation startup, and I'm building a 3 DOF robotic arm and an autonomous rescue drone in my basement. Because apparently I don't sleep.
