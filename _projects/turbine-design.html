MAE 4272 Turbine Design

Designed and wind-tunnel tested a blade turbine using BEM theory. NACA 4412 airfoil, optimized chord and twist distribution via Matlab, then 3D printed and tested across multiple wind speeds.

BEM TheoryMatlabFusion 360NACA 4412Wind TunnelFDM

Workflow

Constraints → Design → CAD → Print → Wind-tunnel test → Data analysis

Constraints

Wind tunnel speed capped at 20 m/s, max rotational speed 3000 RPM, and turbine length under 6 inches to fit the test section. Chord thickness had to be sufficient to avoid brittle failure.

Design

Blade geometry was designed using BEM theory in Matlab. A NACA 4412 airfoil was selected for its low Reynolds number performance. Chord and twist distributions were optimized by looping BEM calculations across a range of tip-speed ratios and selecting the geometry with the highest power output. Optimal design: ~800 RPM at ~6 m/s, TSR ≈ 2.1.

CAD & Print

Blade built in Fusion 360 by importing the NACA 4412 cross-section and lofting airfoil stations along the span using Matlab-generated chord and twist values. Final set 3D printed and assembled onto a hub compatible with the wind tunnel test stand.

Wind Tunnel Testing

Tested at multiple wind speeds using a Pitot-static tube. For each wind speed, torque brake loading was swept until stall, recording wind speed, RPM, and power output to produce power vs. RPM curves and identify peak operating points.

Results

Peak measured power: 0.8335 W at 8.3 m/s and ~1280 RPM (TSR ≈ 2.42). Results compared to BEM predictions and a baseline rotor to evaluate the taper/twist design's effect.

My Contribution

I focused on the modeling and design stage — deriving optimal blade geometry equations, implementing the BEM optimization in Matlab, and performing all post-processing: binning test data by wind speed, extracting peak power points, computing TSR and Cp, generating plots, and estimating long-term average power using a Weibull wind-speed model (k=5, c=5).
