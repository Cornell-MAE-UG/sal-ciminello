Robotic Arm — V4 (in progress)

A self-designed robotic arm, targeting 6 degrees of freedom — mechanical design in Fusion 360, electronics on Raspberry Pi 5, inverse kinematics in Python, 3D printed on a Bambu P1S. Currently finishing mechanical assembly; 3-4 DOF operational so far. All from my basement.

Overview

The V4 robotic arm is the fourth iteration of an ongoing personal project to build a fully functional robotic arm from scratch, targeting 6 degrees of freedom. Assembly is in progress; 3-4 joints are currently operational. Every version has pushed further in mechanical design, control sophistication, and hardware integration.

Hardware

  • Raspberry Pi 5 as the main controller
  • PCA9685 PWM driver over I2C for servo control
  • MG996R servos at the base and shoulder joints
  • SG90/MG90S servos at the distal joints
  • All structural links 3D printed on a Bambu Lab P1S

Software

Control is handled in Python running on the Pi. V4 focuses on implementing proper inverse kinematics so that the arm can be commanded in Cartesian space rather than joint angles — a significant step up from previous versions.

Mechanical Design

All parts designed in Fusion 360. The V4 redesign prioritized maximizing degrees of freedom within the arm's envelope, improving joint rigidity, and making the design more printable without supports.
