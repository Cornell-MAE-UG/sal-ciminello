Autonomous Rescue Drone (in assembly)

A ~24" autonomous quadcopter designed around a life-ring form factor for water rescue. Custom 3D-printed shell over an F450 frame — currently soldering ESC power leads and motor connections, with the power module still to be installed before the frame closes.

ArduPilotPixhawk 6C MiniRaspberry PiMAVLinkDroneKitMAVSDKFusion 360
Full write-up coming soon — build log, CAD, and autonomy stack walkthrough on the way.

Concept

The idea came from thinking about water rescue response time. A drone that can autonomously navigate to a person in distress and deploy a life ring is faster than any human swimmer or boat in most scenarios. This project is the engineering proof-of-concept for that idea.

Hardware Stack

  • F450 frame extended to ~24" diameter with custom 3D printed arm extensions
  • Holybro Pixhawk 6C Mini running ArduPilot
  • Raspberry Pi companion computer for high-level autonomy
  • A2212 brushless motors with BLHeli_S ESCs
  • 4S LiPo power system
  • RadioMaster Pocket ELRS for RC control

Autonomy Stack

Planned architecture: the Raspberry Pi will communicate with the Pixhawk over MAVLink via a serial connection, with high-level mission logic in Python using DroneKit and MAVSDK to handle waypoint navigation, position hold, and autonomous delivery sequences. Software architecture is designed; integration testing begins once hardware assembly is complete.

Mechanical Design

The life-ring form factor required extending the stock F450 frame from ~18" to ~24". Custom arm extension pieces were designed in Fusion 360 and printed on the Bambu P1S to maintain structural stiffness while adding the required diameter.
